# 🧭 Code of Conduct — Lagless Core

## Our Pledge

We, as contributors and maintainers of Lagless Core, pledge to make participation in this project a **respectful, inclusive, and productive experience** for everyone.

We are committed to fostering a community where:

* technical excellence is prioritized
* collaboration is constructive
* disagreements remain professional
* all contributors are treated with dignity

---

# 🌍 Our Standards

## ✔️ Expected Behavior

Participants are expected to:

* Use clear, respectful, and professional communication
* Focus criticism on ideas, not individuals
* Collaborate with intent to improve the system
* Respect architectural decisions and explain improvements clearly
* Provide constructive feedback in reviews and discussions
* Follow project contribution guidelines

---

## ❌ Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated:

* Harassment, insults, or personal attacks
* Disrespectful or dismissive language toward contributors
* Spam, trolling, or disruptive communication
* Dismissing technical contributions without reasoning
* Publishing private discussions without consent
* Any form of discriminatory language or behavior

---

# 🧠 Engineering Culture

Lagless Core is a **systems-level engineering project**, and we value:

* clarity over complexity
* correctness over speed
* design discipline over improvisation
* reproducibility over guesswork

Disagreements are expected, but must remain:

```text id="c1"
technical, evidence-based, and respectful
```

---

# 🔁 Review Philosophy

All reviews should:

* explain reasoning clearly
* avoid emotional or personal framing
* suggest improvements, not just rejections
* respect the contributor’s effort and intent

Example:

✔️ Good:

> “This breaks engine isolation because SyncEngine is directly accessing CacheEngine state. Consider using an event instead.”

❌ Bad:

> “This is wrong, fix it.”

---

# ⚙️ Maintainer Responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for:

* enforcing architectural consistency
* ensuring fair review processes
* maintaining a respectful environment
* de-escalating conflicts when necessary

Maintainers must lead by example in tone and technical clarity.

---

# 📡 Communication Channels

All communication (issues, PRs, discussions) should remain:

* technical
* relevant
* structured

Avoid:

* unrelated debates
* emotional escalation
* off-topic arguments

---

# 🧩 Conflict Resolution

If disagreements arise:

## Step 1: Discussion

Attempt to resolve through technical explanation.

## Step 2: Maintainer review

If unresolved, maintainers will provide final technical judgment.

## Step 3: Escalation

If needed, issues may be temporarily locked to maintain stability.

---

# 🔒 Enforcement

Violations may result in:

* warning
* temporary restriction
* permanent removal from contribution access

All enforcement actions will be based on behavior, not identity or opinion.

---

# 🤝 Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

* GitHub repository interactions
* issue discussions
* pull requests
* community channels (if applicable)
* plugin ecosystem contributions

---

# 🚀 Engineering Ethos

Lagless Core is built on:

```text id="e1"
precision, modularity, and reliability
```

Our community must reflect the same values.

---

# 🧠 Final Note

This is a collaborative engineering environment.

We succeed when:

* communication is clear
* feedback is constructive
* engineering discipline is respected
* everyone contributes to system integrity
